Overview

The AI Governance Copilot is a prototype I built to explore how AI can help compliance and model risk teams at fintechs and regulated financial institutions document, review, and audit AI models more efficiently. The core hypothesis: governance documentation is largely manual, repetitive, and inconsistent — and AI is well-suited to automate exactly those tasks.

Built during the AI Prototyping for PMs course using a vibe-coding approach on Magic Patterns, the prototype evolved into a full PRD with 5 core features, 4 user personas, defined success metrics, and a V1 → V2 roadmap. The tool targets the SR 11-7 regulatory framework — the Federal Reserve's Model Risk Management guidance used by U.S. banking regulators.

The Problem

Fintechs and AI-enabled financial startups are deploying high-impact models without structured, regulator-aligned governance frameworks. As AI adoption accelerates — model inventories are growing 30–50% annually — governance processes remain largely manual, slow, and inconsistent.

Current State

Manual intake → manual documentation → manual review → examiner findings → remediation cycle (6–12 weeks per model)

TARGET STATE

Guided intake → AI-drafted documentation → automated gap scan → pre-reviewed evidence pack (1–2 weeks per model)

01

Documentation Burden

Each model requires 20–40 pages of governance documentation, often written entirely by hand. Model Risk Managers spend 60%+ of their time on documentation quality rather than actual risk analysis.

02

Inconsistent Quality

Documentation varies by author — creating gaps that examiners flag during regulatory review. There's no structured, repeatable process to ensure consistent SR 11-7 coverage across models.

03

Late Risk Detection

Governance gaps are discovered during committee review or regulatory exams — not during model development. This leads to costly remediation cycles and potential MRA/MRIA findings.

04

Scaling Pressure

AI model inventories are growing 30–50% annually as AI adoption accelerates. Teams don't have the bandwidth to manually document every model to the standard regulators expect.

03

The Opportunity

AI is well-suited to automate exactly the kind of structured, repeatable tasks that make governance so burdensome. The Copilot targets four specific workflow improvements where AI provides the highest leverage.

💬

Structured Interviews

AI-guided conversations that collect model information without requiring teams to know SR 11-7 requirements upfront.

📄

Draft Documentation

AI-generated governance docs from interview data — covering all 6 required SR 11-7 sections automatically.

🔍

Risk Identification

Proactive gap detection that flags compliance risks and missing evidence before committee review or regulatory exams.

📦

Review Preparation

Bundled evidence packs ready for internal review boards — fully organized, exportable, and examiner-appropriate.

Why Now

Regulators interpret "models" broadly — anything used to inform or automate decisions falls under MRM expectations. SR 11-7 compliance is increasingly examined for AI/ML systems, especially Tier 1 use cases like underwriting, fraud, and AML.

Market Gap

Existing solutions are enterprise-only and expensive. There's a clear SMB fintech gap — smaller AI-deploying institutions need structured governance tools but can't afford enterprise platforms. High willingness to pay, low competition in the mid-market.

05

End-to-End Workflow

The Copilot is designed as a linear, AI-assisted workflow — each step reduces manual effort and builds toward a complete, examiner-ready evidence pack. Every stage is AI-assisted, not AI-replaced: humans stay in control of final review and sign-off.

1
Model Inventory
User registers or selects an AI model. Tier and status auto-assigned on entry.
2
AI Interview
Conversational intake extracts 7 structured governance fields through natural Q&A.
3
Draft Docs
AI generates all 6 SR 11-7 documentation sections from the intake data.
4
Digital Auditor
AI scans governance state and flags missing evidence, logical gaps, and red flags.
5
Export Pack
Bundled evidence artifact ready for internal review board or regulatory exam.
🔒

Key Design Principle

Tier scoring is fully deterministic and transparent — AI is used only for narrative generation and gap analysis, never for tier assignment. This ensures the governance output is defensible and auditable by regulators.

06

Core Features

The prototype ships 4 modules on the landing page, plus a Governance Gap Scanner. Each module maps to a specific workflow stage and a distinct user pain point.

Module 1

Inventory + Tiering Wizard

Build a structured model inventory record and get a risk-based tier classification based on deterministic scoring.

What it does

  • 6-section intake form (Model Basics, Intended Use, Data Profile, Model Approach, Materiality Signals, Controls Snapshot)
  • Weighted scoring across 5 dimensions: Financial Exposure (30%), Consumer Harm (20%), Regulatory Sensitivity (20%), Model Complexity (15%), Vendor Dependency (15%)
  • Hard overrides: Underwriting → Tier 1, AML → Tier 1, Fraud + decision impact → Tier 1
  • Outputs: Tier badge, risk scoring table, required artifacts checklist, assumptions section
  • Export as simulated PDF evidence pack
Module 2

Conceptual Soundness Builder

Draft SR 11-7 conceptual soundness documentation through a structured intake and AI narrative generation.

What it does

  • 5-section intake: Problem Definition, Approach Rationale, Data & Lineage, Assumptions & Limitations, Operational Boundaries
  • AI generates narrative documentation covering Objective & Target, Approach Rationale, Model Flow Diagram, Assumptions, and Evidence Checklist
  • Pre-filled with sample fraud scoring model ("Sentinel Fraud Scoring") for instant exploration
  • One-click copy or export to evidence pack
Module 3

Regulatory Scrutiny Packet

Generate an examiner-ready governance summary for models currently under or preparing for regulatory review.

What it does

  • Intake covers: Exam Scenario, Model Status, Performance Snapshot, Governance Status, Remediation Plan (up to 3 gaps with owners and target dates)
  • Pre-filled with AML alert prioritization scenario
  • Outputs formal SR 11-7 Alignment Memo: Overview, Validation Summary, Monitoring & Change Management, Issues & Remediation table, Appendix Index
  • Professional, regulator-appropriate tone throughout — no emojis, no gradients
Module 4 — Flagship

Tier Engine + Gap Scanner

Live deterministic risk scoring with full transparency — governance checklists update in real time as inputs change.

What it does

  • 5-step guided intake: Model Basics → Decisioning Context → Exposure → Model & Dependency → Review
  • Live right panel: Risk Score Card (color-coded Tier badge), Tier Classification Logic (threshold table + active overrides), Full Scoring Breakdown (weights, contributions, adjustments)
  • Dynamic governance checklist: Tier 1 = ~17 items, Tier 2 = ~10, Tier 3 = 3. Bias-related items appear only when customer-facing
  • Governance Gap Scanner: paste any governance text → Completeness Score, Maturity Level, Missing Mandatory Components, Remediation Checklist
  • Demo: Switch Business Function from Underwriting to Internal Optimization — score drops, tier changes, override disappears, checklist shrinks — all instantly

Validation Plan

The prototype is designed to be tested with professionals in Model Risk Management, Compliance Teams, AI Governance, and Data Scientists in regulated industries. Three core questions drive the validation strategy.

Q1 Would this reduce time spent creating governance docs? Could you see using this before your next model review cycle?
Q2 Which governance tasks are most manual and time-consuming for your team today?
Q3 Where could AI assistance provide the most value — intake, documentation drafting, or gap identification?

Target Interviewees

Model Risk Management professionals · Compliance Teams · AI Governance Teams · Data Scientists in regulated industries (fintech, banking, insurance)

Next Step

Conduct discovery interviews with governance professionals and iterate on the workflow based on which modules they find most valuable.

10

Reflection

Building the AI Governance Copilot pushed me to think like both a PM and a product designer simultaneously — prototyping fast while making deliberate decisions about where AI should and shouldn't be involved in a regulated workflow.

Key Design Decision

Keeping tier scoring fully deterministic — no AI in the classification step — was the most important product decision I made. In a regulated context, "the AI decided" is not an acceptable governance explanation. Every tier assignment needs to be explainable, auditable, and defensible. AI earns trust through transparency, not automation.

What the Prototype Proved

The vibe-coding approach via Magic Patterns let me move from idea to testable prototype in hours — validating the concept before writing a single line of production code. This is the PM superpower that AI prototyping unlocks: de-risking product bets cheaply and fast.

What I'd Validate Next

The biggest open question is which module provides the most immediate value to users. My hypothesis is the Tier Engine + Gap Scanner — because it's the one place where compliance teams have no good existing tool. I'd run usability sessions with 3–5 Model Risk Managers before building anything else.

Market Strategy Insight

Starting US-only and targeting the $10–20K MRR niche was the right call. Removing UAE from scope early eliminated legal complexity, sharpened the regulatory story (SR 11-7 is well-understood), and gave the prototype a clear, credible anchor. Clarity beats optionality in early-stage product work.
