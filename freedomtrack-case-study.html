Overview

FreedomTrack is an AI-powered personal finance MVP I designed and built from scratch. The core idea: most personal finance apps track your spending — but none connect the dots between your daily financial behavior and your long-term financial independence. FreedomTrack does both, using Google Gemini AI to generate insights, audit subscriptions, stress-test finances, and project a personalized "freedom date" — the day you achieve financial independence.

Built as a hands-on AI prototyping project, FreedomTrack demonstrates full product thinking — from problem definition and user flow design through to a working, feature-complete React application with 15+ distinct AI-powered modules working together as a cohesive product.

The Problem

Personal finance apps are good at recording what happened — but terrible at helping users understand what it means or what to do about it. Most people have no idea how their daily spending decisions connect to their long-term financial future.

📊

Tracking Without Insight

Most apps show you charts of where your money went. None connect spending behavior to long-term financial independence. Users track, but don't change.

🩸

Subscription Drain

Recurring subscriptions silently accumulate. Users routinely pay for services they forgot they had — or have multiple overlapping subscriptions doing the same job.

💳

Debt Without Strategy

Most people pay minimums on debt without a clear payoff strategy. The difference between Snowball and Avalanche approaches can save thousands in interest — but requires calculation most people never do.

🎯

No Freedom Horizon

Nobody knows their financial independence date. Without a concrete target, financial decisions feel abstract. FreedomTrack makes that date real — and shows exactly what changes it.

03

15+ AI-Powered Modules

FreedomTrack is built as a tabbed dashboard with 15+ distinct modules — each addressing a specific financial pain point. Every module uses Google Gemini AI for narrative generation and insight, while keeping deterministic logic (like debt scoring and tier classification) fully transparent and rule-based.

AI Audit

🦇 Vampire Audit

Identifies hidden subscription leaks and redundant recurring expenses that are silently draining the user's finances.

What it does

  • AI scans all recurring expenses and cross-references them for redundancy and unused services
  • Flags subscriptions the user likely forgot about or no longer uses
  • Calculates total annual cost of all subscriptions
  • Generates plain-language audit insight with specific cancellation recommendations
  • Recurring expenses auto-carry over to next budget cycle
Strategy Engine

🏰 Debt Fortress

Snowball vs Avalanche debt payoff strategy engine with a 5-year projection chart and AI strategy intelligence.

What it does

  • Snowball (lowest balance first) vs Avalanche (highest APR first) toggle — updates all calculations instantly
  • 5-year payoff projection chart using AreaChart — shows balance declining to zero over time
  • AI explains which strategy saves more interest and how many months it saves
  • Tracks total debt, minimum monthly payments, and projected payoff date
  • Each debt card shows APR, balance, and minimum payment with strategy highlight
AI Projection

🌅 Freedom Horizon

AI projects the user's financial independence date with a milestone roadmap and confidence-rated targets.

What it does

  • Calculates an estimated "freedom date" — the day the user achieves financial independence based on current trajectory
  • 3 milestone cards with ETA in years/months, confidence level (High/Medium), and specific action items
  • Yearly velocity metric — how much net worth grows per year at current rate
  • Simulated mode available via Wealth Lab — shows how income/expense changes shift the date
Stress Test

🏛️ Economic Fortress

AI stress-tests the user's finances against real-world shock scenarios — job loss, inflation spikes, and emergency events.

What it does

  • Resilience score with 4 levels: Fragile, Stable, Fortified, Unstoppable
  • Runway months — how long current savings would last with no income
  • Two pre-built stress tests: "10% Inflation Spike" and "Income Loss for 3 Months"
  • AI generates a scenario-specific analysis for each stress test
  • Emergency fund status assessment with critical advice
Simulation

🧪 Wealth Lab

Live simulation sliders that model how income increases or expense cuts would change the user's freedom date.

What it does

  • Income Delta slider: -$1,000 to +$5,000 — models effect of raise, side income, or income loss
  • Monthly Burn Cut slider: $0 to $2,000 — models effect of expense reduction
  • Runs AI simulation and updates the Freedom Horizon projection in real time
  • Simulation results are clearly labeled as experimental — not saved to the main projection

💰

Key Design Principle

Google Gemini is used only for narrative generation and insight — not for financial calculations. All deterministic logic (debt scoring, net worth, budget math, resilience scoring) is rule-based and fully transparent. Users can always see exactly how their numbers are calculated.

North Star & Success Metrics

The product is designed around a single north star: getting users to their first meaningful financial insight as fast as possible. Every module is built to deliver an "aha moment" — a realization about their finances they didn't have before.

North Star

How quickly a new user reaches their first financial independence projection — the core value moment of the product.

Leading Indicators

  • Modules explored per user

  • Wealth Lab simulations run

Lagging Indicators

  • Month-over-month net worth change
  • Subscription cancellation rate post-Vampire Audit
  • Debt payoff acceleration
  • Freedom Date movement over time

07

Reflection

Building FreedomTrack from scratch — designing the product, writing the logic, and integrating Gemini AI across 15+ modules — was the most intensive hands-on prototyping experience I've had as a PM. Here's what I learned.

AI Should Explain, Not Decide

The most important design decision in FreedomTrack was keeping all financial math deterministic and rule-based — AI only generates the narrative around those numbers. This made the product more trustworthy and debuggable. Users can always verify the math independently of what the AI says about it.

The Freedom Date is the Product

Every other feature exists to serve one insight: here's when you can be financially free, and here's exactly what changes it. The Wealth Lab simulation was the feature that made this tangible — watching your freedom date move as you adjust income and expenses is genuinely motivating in a way that charts of past spending never are.

Scope Creep is Real — Even Solo

Starting with a simple expense tracker, I ended up with 15+ modules. Each feature made sense in isolation, but the cumulative complexity created UX challenges — users needed guidance on where to start. A stronger onboarding flow that directs users to the 3 most valuable modules first would have improved the product significantly.

What I'd Validate Next

The biggest open question is whether the Vampire Audit or the Freedom Horizon drives more retention. My hypothesis is the Vampire Audit delivers faster initial value (immediate savings) while the Freedom Horizon drives longer-term engagement. I'd run a 2-week cohort study to validate which drives more return visits.
