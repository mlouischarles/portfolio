Overview

This product case study was my final presentation for the General Assembly Product Management course in 2021. The prompt: identify an innovation opportunity for JPMorgan Chase and build a full product case — from market analysis through wireframes, OKRs, and go-to-market strategy.

My proposal: integrate a cryptocurrency e-wallet directly into Chase Pay — allowing Chase's 51 million active digital customers to send, receive, and store crypto without leaving their existing banking app. The case drew on lessons from Kodak and Blockbuster to argue that financial incumbents must embrace crypto now, or risk being disrupted by fintech competitors who already have.

The Innovation Case

The case study opens with a critical lesson: incumbents who ignore emerging technology don't just miss opportunities — they get replaced. Two historical examples anchor the argument for why Chase must act on crypto now.

The Argument

"For JPMorgan Chase not to go down the path of Blockbuster and Kodak, we must embrace innovation. Cryptocurrency is the innovation happening in our industry right now."

Blockbuster had 84,300 employees and 9,094 stores at its peak in 2014. Netflix approached them in 2000 about acquisition — they declined. Filed bankruptcy 2012.

Kodak developed the first digital camera in 1975 — but denied the digital photography revolution for too long out of fear of cannibalizing their film business. A former VP described it as "a cute idea we couldn't get approval to launch."

📱

Crypto Is Legal

The U.S. Treasury classified cryptocurrency as a convertible decentralized virtual currency in 2013. Under federal law, crypto is defined as a commodity. JPMorgan already spends ~$11B/year on blockchain technology.

🏦

Banks Are At Risk

Both financial institutions and non-banking competitors face disruption risk from crypto — especially technologies that require no intermediation. Fintech firms closed the gap rapidly during the pandemic.

⚡

The Window Is Now

The current crypto asset market cap was well over $272 billion at the time of the case study. PayPal and Square already surpassed traditional banking competitors in market cap. First-mover advantage in banking crypto is still available.

03

The Problem

Chase customers who want to use cryptocurrency have no way to do so within their existing banking relationship — forcing them to use separate apps and services, fragmenting their financial life and driving engagement to competitors.

Problem Statement

"I need a way to transfer cryptocurrencies in the United States and overseas to other people using the Chase Pay mobile app because I need a secure way to complete these transactions. I would like to save money on fees when I complete a transaction."

01

Customer Demand Is Growing

There is increasing customer demand globally — more people are using cryptocurrencies as an alternative way to transact, especially in unstable currency environments. Chase clients are moving assets to Coinbase, Cash App, and PayPal to access crypto.

02

Fee Savings Opportunity

Users want to save on transaction fees when sending money domestically and internationally. Crypto-native transfers — especially for remittances to Africa, the UK, and beyond — can significantly reduce the fee burden compared to traditional wire transfers.

03

No Centralized Crypto Banking

Chase customers currently need to manage their banking and crypto assets across multiple separate platforms. There is no integrated view of their total financial picture in one trusted place.

04

Competitive Erosion Risk

Amazon, Apple, Square, and PayPal are all major risks to traditional banking in payments. Without a crypto offering, Chase risks losing digital-first customers to fintech competitors who have already built crypto capabilities.

The Solution

The proposed solution integrates cryptocurrency e-wallets directly into Chase's existing mobile banking platform — giving customers a central, trusted place to manage both traditional banking and crypto assets without switching apps.

"The feature of being able to send cryptocurrency to my family and friends in London and Africa. I want something secure and saving on fees would be great."

Client Survey — MP

"Ability to exchange crypto as easily as cash person to person, without fees and the same level of privacy."

Client Survey — DNF

👛

Crypto E-Wallet Integration

Connecting popular crypto wallets directly inside the Chase mobile app — covering the most widely used wallets. Users can store, send, and receive crypto alongside their existing Chase accounts.

  • Multi-wallet support for major crypto ecosystems
  • Send crypto to any Chase customer by phone number
  • International crypto transfers with minimal fees
📊

Blockchain Data Visibility

Users can view real-time blockchain mining data, transaction history, and market context directly within the app — giving them the information they need to make informed decisions without leaving Chase.

  • Transaction history and blockchain verification
  • Aggregate crypto news feed
  • Real-time market data and portfolio view
📰

Crypto News Aggregation

Active crypto users want to stay informed. An integrated news feed surfaces relevant crypto industry news, keeping Chase customers engaged within the platform rather than turning to third-party apps for information.

🔢

Fractional Share Buying

Users want the option to buy fractional shares of certain cryptocurrencies — lowering the barrier to entry for customers who want to invest in Bitcoin or Ethereum without purchasing a full unit.

07

Target Audience

The initial rollout targets a specific segment of Chase's existing 51 million digital customers — not the crypto-native power users, but the broader mainstream adopters who are crypto-curious and need a trusted, low-friction entry point.

🎯

Demographics

Tech-savvy customers in the 25–45 age range — comfortable with mobile banking and open to emerging financial technologies, but not yet engaged with standalone crypto apps.

📈

Adoption Profile

Focused on early adopters to late majority crypto users — deliberately avoiding the crypto-native "innovator" segment who have already established crypto relationships elsewhere.

🌍

Use Cases

Customers with international remittance needs (US, UK, Africa, Mexico, China) who want to send money abroad more cheaply, plus domestic peer-to-peer crypto transfers.

Reflection

This case study was one of my first full PM deliverables — and building it under a General Assembly course deadline taught me how to move fast from market insight to product recommendation with a coherent narrative.

The Innovation Framing

Opening with Blockbuster and Kodak was a deliberate rhetorical choice — it anchored the product recommendation in a widely understood business risk. For a PM working inside a large institution, framing a new feature as "defense against disruption" is often more persuasive than framing it as "new revenue opportunity."

Leverage Existing Assets

The strongest part of the Chase case was the distribution argument: 51 million active digital customers already trust Chase with their money. A crypto wallet doesn't need to win users from scratch — it just needs to activate an existing relationship. That's a very different GTM challenge than a standalone crypto app faces.

What I'd Do Differently

I'd spend more time on the regulatory risk section — which was light in the original deck. Crypto integration in a federally regulated bank involves significant compliance complexity (BSA/AML, FinCEN reporting, custody rules) that a real product proposal would need to address head-on rather than assume away.

What This Built

This was where I learned to structure a product argument: problem → market → competition → solution → audience → metrics → OKRs. That scaffold is something I've carried into every subsequent case study and PRD I've written since — the logic and flow from this first attempt became a template.
