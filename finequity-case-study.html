Overview

finEQUITY is a Brooklyn-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit that provides financial tools, coaching, and educational resources specifically designed for individuals impacted by incarceration. Their mission is to eliminate the financial barriers that prevent justice-impacted people from rebuilding their lives — from credit building to no-fee banking to financial coaching.

I served as PM Co-lead on a cross-functional product team working alongside Ademola Adekemi. In this role, I coordinated team alignment through weekly ceremonies, translated high-level organizational strategy into actionable team objectives, streamlined delivery processes, and led demo presentations showcasing the team's contributions to finEQUITY's product goals.

Mission & Vision

Organization Mission

"Build a brighter financial future. finEQUITY's resources are thoughtfully designed with your financial health in mind."

Who They Serve

Justice-impacted individuals — people who have been affected by incarceration and face systemic financial barriers including lack of access to credit, banking, and financial education. finEQUITY meets them where they are.

Why It Matters

Financial exclusion is one of the most significant barriers to successful reentry. Without access to banking, credit, and financial tools, individuals face compounding disadvantage. finEQUITY addresses this gap head-on with practical tools and human coaching.

03

The Problem Space

Justice-impacted individuals face a compounding set of financial barriers that make economic stability extremely difficult to achieve after incarceration. finEQUITY was built to address each of these barriers directly.

01

No Credit History

Incarceration often means years of no credit activity. Without a credit score, individuals are shut out of housing, employment opportunities, and basic financial services that most people take for granted.

02

Banking Exclusion

Traditional banks frequently deny accounts to people with criminal records. This forces reliance on check-cashing services and prepaid cards with high fees — a poverty tax on an already vulnerable population.

03

Financial Literacy Gap

Financial education in correctional settings is minimal. Upon release, individuals must navigate complex financial systems — credit, banking, budgeting — with little preparation or support infrastructure.

Programs & Products

finEQUITY delivers financial empowerment through three core program offerings, each designed to meet participants where they are — whether inside or after incarceration — and move them toward lasting financial freedom.

💳

Credit Building Strategies

Personalized credit building plans, guidance on secured credit products, and ongoing coaching to help participants establish and grow their credit scores from zero.

🏦

No-Fee Bank Account Access

finEQUITY connects participants with banking partners offering no-fee accounts — removing the banking exclusion barrier that traps many justice-impacted individuals in high-cost alternatives.

📖

Financial Education Curriculum

A correspondence course delivered to New York State prisons via postal mail — covering financial strategies, budgeting, and planning — reaching participants even before release.

🏆

Prison 2 Prosperity Rewards Program

A milestone rewards program for successful participants — incentivizing financial behavior change with tangible rewards for hitting savings, credit, and educational milestones.

👥

Financial Coaching

One-on-one coaching from certified coaches who understand the unique challenges facing justice-impacted individuals — providing personalized guidance and accountability.

🎓

Virtual Credit Workshops

Group virtual workshops on credit, banking, and financial planning — delivered in partnership with sponsor organizations and accessible to participants at all financial literacy levels.

Reflection

Working as PM Co-lead on finEQUITY was one of the most meaningful product experiences I've had — not just for the skill-building, but for the mission itself. Here's what I took away.

Leading Without Hierarchy

As Co-lead, I had to earn alignment through clarity and communication, not authority. Building consensus on objectives with a distributed team — including apprentices who were newer to PM — sharpened my facilitation and coaching instincts significantly.

Mission-Driven Product Work

Building product for a social impact organization changes how you prioritize. Every feature decision had a real human consequence — not just a metric. That forces a kind of PM rigor that purely commercial products don't demand in the same way.

Translating Strategy to Action

One of my core contributions was translating finEQUITY's high-level organizational goals into concrete, sprint-ready team objectives. That translation layer — from vision to backlog — is where a lot of PM value lives, and this experience sharpened that skill.

Cross-Functional Communication

Coordinating demo presentations to organizational stakeholders required distilling complex cross-team work into a clear, compelling narrative. Learning to communicate progress — not just status — to a mission-focused leadership team was a valuable and distinct communication challenge.
