Overview

Rentwise Austin is an AI-powered rent affordability calculator designed specifically for first-time renters navigating the Austin, TX rental market. Users enter their monthly income, debt payments, fixed expenses, and savings goals — and Gemini AI returns Conservative, Moderate, and Stretch rent budget recommendations with Austin-specific market context, a full budget snapshot, action items, and follow-up question prompts.

This project was built as part of the In the Lab PM Bootcamp — a competitive 13-week program for underrepresented talent in product management, culminating in a final presentation to a panel of industry product leaders. Deliverables included a one-pager, full PRD, FigJam user flow, 3-year product roadmap, go-to-market strategy, and a working prototype.

02

The Problem

Problem Statement: First-time renters need a way to understand what they can realistically afford because searching for housing without financial clarity leads to rent burden, poor decisions, and long-term financial stress. Users need a way to access their financial information — income, expenses, and savings goals — in one place and receive clear, personalized guidance.

01

No Clear Affordability Guidance

Most renters use rough rules of thumb like "30% of income on rent" — but this ignores debt payments, savings goals, and actual cost of living in Austin, which varies dramatically by neighborhood.

02

Rent Burden & Financial Stress

Without personalized guidance, first-time renters routinely overspend on housing. This leads to financial stress, reduced savings, and difficulty building long-term financial stability.

03

Low Financial Literacy

Young renters — especially students and recent graduates — lack the financial planning foundation to evaluate rental decisions confidently. Key concepts like DTI ratio and cost of living benchmarks are unfamiliar.

04

Austin-Specific Complexity

Austin's rental market has its own dynamics — from rapid price changes to neighborhood cost variation. Generic national tools don't account for local nuance that significantly affects affordability calculations.

03

Hypothesis

Product Hypothesis

"Providing first-time renters with an accessible and user-friendly online tool that calculates their affordable rent based on income, expenses, and location-specific data will lead to increased financial literacy and better-informed renting decisions. We hypothesize that such a tool will empower first-time renters to make more informed choices, reducing the risk of rent burden and financial stress."

Empowerment

Renters take control of their financial future and housing choices with data, not guesswork.

Reduced Stress

Accurate information and guidance reduce the risk of rent burden and financial anxiety.

Financial Literacy

Educational content equips users with essential life skills beyond just this rental decision.

No Commitment

Low-pressure access to financial clarity without committing to any property or landlord.

Core Features

The PRD defines four core features — scoped for the MVP with a clear in/out-of-scope boundary to keep the first version focused and shippable.

🧮

Affordability Calculator

Takes monthly income, debt payments, fixed expenses, and savings goal as inputs. AI calculates the maximum affordable rent and returns three budget tiers (Conservative / Moderate / Stretch) with personalized context. Users can save and revisit previous results.

📚

Financial Literacy Hub

Articles, videos, and interactive tools covering budgeting fundamentals, financial planning, and key rental terms. Surfaced to all users regardless of their calculator results — education is always available, not gated on outcomes.

📍

Austin Location Insights

Users can search specific Austin neighborhoods and receive data on cost of living, average rents, and local amenities. Helps users match their budget tier to realistic neighborhood options in the Austin market.

🎯

Personalized Recommendations

Based on the user's financial situation, budget tier, and preferences — the platform surfaces housing options and next-step guidance. Recommendations factor in budget, location, bedroom count, and stated amenity priorities.

Product Scope

The PRD defines a clear in-scope / out-of-scope boundary to keep the MVP tightly focused on core value — the affordability calculator — without distraction from payment features or third-party integrations.

✓ In Scope — MVP

Affordability Calculator (income + expense inputs → rent tiers)
Austin-specific housing resources and financial literacy content
Location insights (cost of living, average rents, amenities)
Personalized recommendations based on user financial profile
User accounts with saved calculation history
Mobile-responsive, accessible UI
Save and print affordability results

✕ Out of Scope

Payment processing or transactional capabilities
Integration with external financial or real estate services
Landlord-facing features or listing management
Credit score retrieval or financial account linking
Lease signing or tenant application flows
Multi-city support (Austin-only for V1)

Go-to-Market Strategy

The GTM strategy targets first-time renters through three channels: institutional partnerships, content marketing, and community-driven growth — with a freemium model anchored on the calculator and premium resources behind a low-cost paywall.

01

Pre-Launch

Social media teasing, targeted ads, and a beta testing group from Austin's student and young professional community. Build anticipation before the official launch with a countdown and early access list.

02

Partnerships

Establish partnerships with real estate agencies, housing providers, educational institutions, and community housing organizations in Austin to expand reach and add credibility. Cross-promotion and co-marketing.

03

Content & Community

Blog posts, videos, and infographics on renting, financial literacy, and Austin housing trends. Active social media presence. Email campaigns segmented by user behavior. Forums for renters to share experiences.

Revenue Model

Free Tier

Full access to the affordability calculator — the core value proposition, always free. No commitment required.

Premium Tier

Small monthly fee unlocks financial literacy resources, saved calculation history, location insights, and personalized recommendations.

12

Reflection

Rentwise was my most complete end-to-end PM exercise — from problem discovery through a working AI prototype. Building every deliverable in the same 13-week bootcamp that covered the theory behind them accelerated my learning significantly.

Biggest PM Lesson

Scoping ruthlessly is a product skill, not a limitation. Keeping Rentwise Austin-only and building a free calculator before any premium features forced clarity on what the core value proposition actually was. Every constraint became a sharper decision.

What the Prototype Proved

Building the working AI prototype validated that the core UX — enter income, get three clear budget tiers with context — was immediately understandable without any onboarding. The Gemini output was specific enough to be useful and conservative enough to be trustworthy.

What I'd Validate Next

The biggest open question is whether the three-tier output (Conservative / Moderate / Stretch) is the right framing, or whether users want a single recommended number with a range. I'd run 5 usability sessions with real first-time Austin renters before committing to the output format.

In the Lab Impact

The bootcamp's structure — learning a PM concept one week, applying it to a real product the next — compressed months of on-the-job learning into 13 weeks. Presenting to a panel of product leaders at the final session was the most valuable feedback experience I've had as a PM.
